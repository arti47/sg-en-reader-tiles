## 1. Project Overview

**What we are building.** A self-adapting English tutor for **Singapore Primary 1‚Äì6 (ages ~7‚Äì12)**, modeled on the adaptive/blended engine of Lexia Core5 but **localised to the Singapore MOE curriculum and PSLE**, for **personal/home use with a small group of children**.

**Primary audience: students with literacy difficulties (incl. dyslexia).** Confirmed profile: **decoding + spelling difficulties (reading and spelling)**. This is the central design driver. Consequences (enforced throughout): Structured Literacy / Orton-Gillingham is **mandatory, not optional**; **every phonics pattern is taught for BOTH reading (decoding) and spelling (encoding)**; **reading level is decoupled from P-level** (a P5 child may need P1‚Äì2 decoding); pacing is **slower with smaller steps and more review**; content is **decodable/controlled** (¬ß6a); UI is **dyslexia-friendly by default** (¬ß14); tone foregrounds **growth over exam pressure** (¬ß10).

**Add-Student is a first-class in-app feature** (not hardcoded profiles): the parent adds a child, sets **chronological P-level**, and the app then runs an **independent reading placement** (¬ß7). Optional difficulty flags captured at add-time tune starting focus.

**Two things Core5's model gives us, kept:** (a) an adaptive engine with continuous embedded assessment ("Assessment Without Testing"), and (b) a blended loop that branches to explicit re-teaching when a child struggles. **What we change:** the *content and assessment* are rebuilt around the MOE EL Syllabus 2020 strands and PSLE Paper components, in **British/Singapore English**, for a **largely bilingual (English + Mother Tongue) learner population**.

**The core loop.**
1. Each child has a profile and a placement on a fixed **P1‚ÜíP6 scope-and-sequence**.
2. The app presents short, adaptive **activities** using **PSLE-style item types**.
3. Every answer is **continuously scored (deterministic)** ‚Üí per-skill **mastery estimate** (no separate test).
4. On mastery ‚Üí advance; on struggle ‚Üí branch to an explicit **Lesson**, then extra **Practice**.
5. A **parent dashboard** shows progress, an **action plan**, and a **PSLE-readiness indicator** framed on the AL1‚ÄìAL8 scale.

**The key architectural bet: FULLY OFFLINE, ZERO RUNTIME AI.**
- **No network access and no AI calls at runtime.** All content ships as **static, pre-authored content packs** (JSON + audio assets) bundled with the app.
- Content packs are **authored/pre-generated at BUILD time** (curated word lists for phonics/spelling; AI may assist authoring offline before shipping, but the shipped app is static).
- **All scoring is deterministic** (exact-match / rule-based). No LLM scoring. Free-text comprehension is deferred or MCQ-only.
- **Lessons are pre-written** per skill and shipped in the pack.
- Rationale: the MVP is decoding+spelling, whose item space is finite and enumerable ‚Äî hardcoded curated lists give guaranteed decodability, correct keys, zero cost, and permanent offline operation on iPad/iPhone.

## 3. Pedagogical Foundation

Two frameworks combine: **MOE EL Syllabus 2020 / STELLAR 2.0** (the *what* ‚Äî content, strands, values) and **Structured Literacy** (the *how* ‚Äî the adaptive/explicit engine, especially for lower-primary foundations).

**Structured Literacy principles ‚Üí features:**

| Principle | Meaning | Where in app |
|---|---|---|
| **Explicit** | Rules taught directly, not guessed | A skill's lesson fires on FIRST exposure (before any test item), then again on struggle (¬ß8) |
| **Systematic** | Simple ‚Üí complex, valid order | Fixed P1‚ÜíP6 scope-and-sequence (¬ß4) |
| **Cumulative** | New builds on prior; constant review | Interleaved review / spaced repetition (¬ß7) |
| **Diagnostic** | Continuously find each child's gaps | Assessment Without Testing (¬ß9) |
| **Responsive** | Adjust in real time | Adaptive engine (¬ß7) |

**Strands (localised).** The MOE syllabus organises learning into Listening & Viewing, Reading & Viewing, Speaking & Representing, Writing & Representing, and **Grammar & Vocabulary** as knowledge about language. For an auto-adaptive app we implement these **six teachable strands**, weighted toward what PSLE Paper 2 actually tests:

1. **Phonics & Word Recognition (decoding)** ‚Äî sound‚Äìletter correspondences, decodable words, high-frequency words. **MVP core.** *Does NOT taper by grade ‚Äî serve by reading placement, not P-level.*
2. **Spelling (encoding)** ‚Äî the reverse operation of every phonics pattern: hear word ‚Üí build/type it. Taught in lockstep with strand 1 (OG: read it AND spell it before mastery). Includes dictation of decodable words/sentences. **MVP core.**
3. **Grammar** ‚Äî tenses, subject‚Äìverb agreement, prepositions, connectors, pronouns, articles, reported speech, verbs of perception, gerunds. *Major PSLE weight; post-MVP emphasis.*
4. **Vocabulary** ‚Äî word meaning, nuance, phrasal verbs, collocations, idioms, context clues.
5. **Cloze** ‚Äî grammar cloze (word-bank) and comprehension cloze (open). *Own strand; upper-primary.*
6. **Comprehension** ‚Äî literal ‚Üí inferential ‚Üí evaluative; visual text; open-ended technique.
7. **Sentence Manipulation** ‚Äî Synthesis & Transformation; Editing (spelling & grammar).

**EAL / bilingual reality.** English is the medium of instruction but often **not the home language** (many children speak Mandarin, Malay, or Tamil at home). Do **not** assume English L1: gloss harder vocabulary, keep instructions simple, and let vocabulary support scaffold meaning.

**Motivation as a feature.** Learner agency + growth mindset; celebrate mastery with certificates carrying **"I can‚Ä¶" statements**; encouraging, non-punitive feedback; short sessions.

## 4. Localisation & PSLE Alignment (READ ‚Äî drives content generation and scoring)

**Language conventions (mandatory in every generated item and in scoring):**
- **British/Singapore English spelling** ‚Äî colour, favourite, realise, centre, travelled, neighbour. **Never** mark US spellings as the *only* correct form; never generate US spellings as targets.
- **Singapore context** ‚Äî local names (e.g., Wei Ming, Siti, Kavitha, Mr Tan), settings (hawker centre, MRT, void deck, HDB, wet market, CCA), currency in dollars/cents. Keep culturally neutral and inclusive across races.
- **Register** ‚Äî "internationally acceptable English that is grammatical and appropriate"; avoid Singlish as a *target answer* (may appear only as an editing/error example to correct).

**PSLE English structure the app builds toward** (from the SEAB PSLE English syllabus; use for component coverage and the readiness indicator, not for exact mark replication):

| Paper | Component | Format | In-app coverage |
|---|---|---|---|
| **P1 Writing (25%)** | Situational + Continuous Writing | open | **Later/optional** (¬ß2) |
| **P2 Language Use & Comprehension (45%)** | Grammar MCQ | MCQ | Grammar strand |
| | Vocabulary MCQ | MCQ | Vocabulary strand |
| | Vocabulary Cloze | MCQ | Vocabulary strand |
| | Visual Text Comprehension | MCQ | Comprehension strand |
| | Grammar Cloze (word bank) | open | Cloze strand |
| | Editing (spelling & grammar) | open | Sentence-manipulation strand |
| | Comprehension Cloze (no word bank) | open | Cloze strand ‚Äî acceptable-list scored |

Grading is reported as **Achievement Levels AL1 (best) ‚Üí AL8**. The readiness indicator (¬ß10) is framed on this scale.

**Support-programme analogy (validates the tiered model).** MOE runs Learning Support Programme (P1‚Äì2), Empowered to Read Too (P1‚Äì2), and School-based Dyslexia Remediation (P3‚Äì4). Our struggle-branch (Lesson ‚Üí Practice) mirrors this "extra explicit support when stuck" idea.

**Suggested progression by strand (compress/expand as needed):**

**Authoring rule:** ship a complete sequence for **Phonics + Spelling** first (the Phase-1 MVP core), with a starter Grammar/Vocab track for variety. PSLE Paper-2 strands (cloze, comprehension, synthesis) follow in M3 ‚Äî they are unreachable for a child who cannot yet decode them.

## 6. Content Model (item types)

Each skill declares an `itemType`. Renderers to implement:

| itemType | Renders | Answer | Scoring |
|---|---|---|---|
| `decode_choice` | Word + audio "read this" + choices, or match word to picture | tap | exact |
| `sight_word_flash` | Word shown briefly; pick from choices | tap, timed | accuracy + latency |
| `grammar_mcq` | Sentence with 4 options (the PSLE grammar MCQ) | tap | exact |
| `vocab_mcq` | Sentence; pick best-meaning word / closest synonym of an underlined word | tap | exact |
| `vocab_cloze_mcq` | Short passage, blanks, MCQ options | tap per blank | exact |
| `grammar_cloze` | Passage with blanks + a **lettered word bank**, each used once | assign words | exact |
| `editing` | Passage with underlined errors; supply correction | short text | exact vs authored key |
| `comprehension_cloze` | Passage, blanks, **no word bank** | short text per blank | matches authored `acceptable[]` lists |
| `synthesis_transform` | Rewrite/combine sentence(s) in required form | text | matches authored `acceptable[]` patterns (deferred if too brittle) |
| `visual_text` | Generated poster/advert description + question | MCQ | exact |
| `passage_question` | Short passage + question | **MCQ only** (free-text deferred ‚Äî no AI scorer) | exact |
| `build_word` | Hear word (audio) ‚Üí drag letter/grapheme tiles to spell it | ordered tiles | exact + **error analysis** (which grapheme missed ‚Üí missedConcept) |
| `spell_tiles` | Hear word ‚Üí build from letter/grapheme tile tray | ordered tiles | exact + error analysis |
| `dictation` | Hear short decodable sentence ‚Üí build word-by-word from tile trays | ordered tiles per word | per-word scoring; rule-based error analysis |

### 6a. Decodability constraint (mandatory, enforced at AUTHORING time)
Every word/sentence in a skill's item pool must be decodable using only patterns taught at or before that skill, plus that skill's allowed high-frequency word list. Enforced by a **build-time lint script** that parses each word against the taught-pattern set and fails the build on violations. Each item carries `decodableWithin: skillId` metadata.

### 6b. Content pack QA (build-time, replaces runtime validator)
A build script validates every pack: (1) answer keys resolve, (2) exactly one correct answer per item, (3) en-SG spelling (dictionary check), (4) decodability lint (¬ß6a), (5) no duplicate item IDs, (6) minimum pool size per skill (‚â•20 items/difficulty where feasible). CI-style: build fails on any violation.

### 6c. Audio strategy (fully offline)
iOS `speechSynthesis` with an **on-device en-GB voice** for words/sentences ‚Äî works offline. Isolated phonemes (/sh/, /a/) cannot be TTS'd reliably: bundle a **pre-recorded phoneme set (~44 clips)** as static assets. `audio.phoneme(id)` plays clips; `audio.speak(text)` uses TTS. One service interface; assets cached by the service worker.

### 6d. Item pools & variety (replaces infinite generation)
Each skill ships a **pool of 20‚Äì40 hand-curated items per difficulty**. Runtime variety via: shuffle, distractor rotation (distractor sets stored per item, sampled), and never repeating an item within its pool cycle. Pool exhaustion recycles with reshuffle ‚Äî acceptable given spaced repetition spreads exposure.

> **Owner directive (2026-07-20) ‚Äî MAX OUT EVERY PHONICS PACK.** For all phonics/spelling packs (T01‚ÄìT12: CVC, digraphs, **blends**, FLOSS, silent-e, vowel teams, r-controlled, diphthongs, two-syllable, HF sets), author to the **quality ceiling** of that pattern (as many decodable, en-SG, child-safe words as the pattern cleanly yields ‚Äî typically ~60 items/skill), not merely the ¬ß6d floor. Applies to every future pack **when building blends and beyond** ‚Äî always go to max before requesting owner sign-off. Precedent: CVC 60, digraphs 60.

**Multisensory (OG requirement):** pair sound + visual + motor (tile drag, typing) for every phonics/spelling pattern.

## 7. Adaptive Engine

**Mastery model.** Rolling-window accuracy per (child, skill), gated by `minItems`. (Interface kept clean so a Bayesian/BKT upgrade can drop in later.)

**Difficulty (1‚Äì3) within a skill.** Start at 1. Correct streak of 3 ‚Üí +1 (cap 3); 2 wrong in recent window ‚Üí ‚àí1 (floor 1). Difficulty flows into generation (harder distractors, subtler nuance, longer/lower-frequency vocabulary, PSLE-length passages at level 3).

**Dual mastery gates (phonics patterns).** A pattern is mastered only when BOTH its decode skill and its encode (spelling) skill pass. Encode unlocks after decode reaches ~70% over **‚â•6 real items** (`ENCODE_UNLOCK_MIN`, so a 3/3 fluke doesn't open spelling early), but advancement requires both. **Massed practice first:** threading + interleave are suppressed until the child's first pattern is acquired. `minItems` for foundational decode/encode is **12** (overlearning for struggling readers; 85%√ó12 ‚âà 11/12).

**Mastery = RETENTION, not acquisition (OG, 2026-07-21).** Passing the dual gate in a session is **PROVISIONAL mastery**: it advances the ladder (keeps momentum) but the **certificate is WITHHELD** until the **first +2d spaced-review pass** confirms retention. So progress `status:'mastered'` = acquisition (drives advancement + the dashboard growth count); a **certificate = retained** (minted on the confirming review). Prevents the "certify now, maybe demote later" false-positive.

**Spaced repetition (replaces vague "review %").** On acquisition, schedule reviews at **+2d, +7d, +21d** for **BOTH the decode and encode skills** of the pattern. Session composition: due reviews first (cap ~4/session), then current-skill items. The certificate is minted only once **both channels have retained** (each review stage ‚â•1 ‚Äî so spelling retention is verified, not assumed). A **failed review truly demotes**: it re-books the near review AND fires an immediate guided re-practice block (3 difficulty-1 items on the forgotten skill), not just a re-test in 2 days. Post-M4: tune intervals.

**Error taxonomy (missedConcept).** Fixed enum per strand ‚Äî e.g. phonics: `digraph-sh`, `vowel-short-a`, `consonant-b` (b/d reversal), `r-controlled-ar`; spelling: `silent-e-a`, `vowel-team-choice`; grammar: `sva-neither-nor`. Drives struggle detection, Lesson targeting, and dashboard "stuck on" reporting. **Grapheme-level (2026-07-21):** for encode items, `scoring.tileMissedConcept` derives the tag from the EXPECTED grapheme at the first same-length SUBSTITUTION via `src/data/graphemeConcepts.json` (`gen-grapheme-concepts.mjs`), so a vowel confusion / digraph swap / b-d reversal is diagnosed precisely; length-mismatch errors (omission/insertion, e.g. a dropped silent-e) fall back to the item's authored `missedConceptOnFail`. MCQ/decode items keep their authored tag.

**Progression pseudocode:**
```
onAnswer(child, skill, item, result):
  record(result)                              # feeds AWT + mastery
  updateDifficulty(child, skill, result)
  m = rollingAccuracy(child, skill, window)
  n = itemsAnswered(child, skill)
  if n >= skill.mastery.minItems and m >= skill.mastery.accuracyToPass:
      markProvisionalMastery(child, skill)    # advances the ladder now‚Ä¶
      scheduleReview(child, skill, +2d)       # ‚Ä¶cert WITHHELD until this review passes (¬ß retention)
      advance(child, nextUnlockedSkill(child))   # respects prereqs
  elif strugglingSignal(child, skill):        # m < 0.6 over >= 5 items, or 2 same-concept misses
      branchToLesson(child, skill)            # may re-fire once more (cap 2/skill/session) if still failing
  else:
      continuePractice(child, skill)
```

**Struggle branching (blended-model equivalent):** re-teach EARLY to minimise error exposure ‚Äî Lesson (explicit rule + worked examples + guided items) ‚Üí Practice (easier extra items) ‚Üí resume. The lesson may re-fire **once more** (cap 2/skill/session, after ‚â•3 further failed attempts) if the child is still struggling.

**Explicit-first teaching (¬ß3, 2026-07-22).** The FIRST time the child meets a new pattern (no prior attempts on that skill, not placement-credited), its lesson is shown **before** any test item ‚Äî teach the rule, don't make them discover it. The shared lesson‚Üíguided-block(we-do)‚Üínormal-items(you-do) flow then gives the full gradual release. A per-skill `introRef` guards it (fires once, and never consumes the struggle re-teach budget); the reactive struggle‚Üílesson branch still fires later if needed.

**Immediate error correction (OG, 2026-07-21).** At the item level, a WRONG answer is scored (assessment) but does NOT end the item: every renderer reveals + **voices** the correct answer and requires the child to **reproduce it correctly** (tap the right choice / rebuild the modelled word / re-place the right cloze word) before continuing ‚Äî so the last production is correct, not the error (avoids consolidating the wrong form). `onAnswer` is deferred until the fix, carrying the first attempt, so score + correct-only fluency latency are unaffected; the placement warm-up (`quiet`) stays assessment-only.

**Placement (dual ‚Äî critical for this audience).** Capture **chronological P-level** at add-time (sets PSLE-component expectations), then run an **independent reading placement** that finds true instructional level per strand by walking **down** on failure / **up** on success. For struggling readers these diverge ‚Äî place content by **reading level, not P-level**. Start decoding placement low and confirm foundations before advancing. Keep placement short (‚â§ ~15 items) and low-pressure: **frame it as a warm-up game, show no right/wrong feedback during placement**, end on an achievable item. **Spelling is NOT free-placed (2026-07-21):** the warm-up only tests decoding, so a high placement credits the lower rungs' decode+encode but **holds back the top TWO read rungs' spelling** ‚Äî the child must earn those in-session before advancing (`encodeUnlocked` treats a placement-confirmed decoder as ‚â•70%). Spelling lags decoding in dyslexia, so a decode-only placement never assumes more than two rungs of spelling. Placement itself needs **3/3 per level** (not 2) so lucky guesses can't over-place.

**Interleaving:** ~15‚Äì20% of session items are quick reviews of mastered skills (cumulative principle).

```ts
};
type PackItem = {
  id:string; skillId:string; itemType:ItemType; difficulty:1|2|3;
  stem:string; passage?:string; displayWord?:string;
  audioText?:string;                 // what TTS speaks (or phonemeId)
  choices?:{id:string;label:string}[];
  distractorPool?:string[];          // rotated at runtime for variety
  wordBank?:string[]; blanks?:{id:string;acceptable:string[]}[];
  correctChoiceId?:string; acceptableAnswers?:string[];
  missedConceptOnFail:string;        // from error taxonomy enum
  decodableWithin:string;            // skillId envelope (¬ß6a)
  rationale:string;                  // shown as feedback/teaching note
};
```

**Authoring pipeline (build time, not runtime):**
1. Curated word lists per phonics/spelling pattern (source: standard OG/decodable lists, adapted to en-SG spelling).
2. Items assembled by script from word lists (decode_choice, build_word, spell_typed, dictation are template-driven ‚Äî cheap to mass-produce deterministically).
3. Grammar/vocab MCQ and later-strand passages: authored with AI assistance **offline during development**, then human-reviewed, then frozen into packs.
4. ¬ß6b QA lint gates every pack into the build.
5. **Owner review gate:** every pack is delivered to the owner as readable JSON (+ a human-readable word-list summary) for review/edit BEFORE freezing into the app bundle. No pack ships unreviewed.

**Scoring (all deterministic):** exact match for MCQ/tiles; tile-sequence exact match for spelling; per-word tile match for dictation; cloze blanks match `acceptable[]` lists (author multiple acceptable answers where genuinely valid). Error analysis = rule-based: compare wrong grapheme/word against `missedConceptOnFail` + positional diff.

## 19. Learn / Test Dual-Mode ‚Äî Structured Learning Path (SPEC ‚Äî owner-approved 2026-07-22, NOT yet built)

> **For the AI agent picking this up:** this is a full, self-contained spec for milestone **M5**. Read it top to bottom. It builds *on top of* the existing engine (¬ß7), lessons (¬ß8), placement (¬ß7), scope & sequence (¬ß5), and item renderers (¬ß6) ‚Äî reuse them, do not rebuild them. Every design choice below was decided with the owner; do not re-litigate them. Build in the ¬ß19.9 phase order, each phase a separate commit with `npm test` + `npm run build` green and a CLAUDE.md changelog row (per ¬ß18).

### 19.1 Why (rationale)
Today the single adaptive session (`Session.tsx`) both **teaches** (lessons fire on first exposure + on struggle) and **assesses** (mastery, SRS, readiness) in one blended loop. The owner wants these split into two deliberate, child-selectable modes so the *teaching* is explicit, systematic and unhurried (the OG ideal for dyslexia) and the *assessment* is clean:
- **Learn mode** ‚Äî a structured, linear, low-pressure path that **teaches** each phonics/spelling pattern (read + spell together). No adaptive difficulty, no mastery pressure, no timing. This becomes the app's explicit-instruction spine.
- **Test mode** ‚Äî the existing adaptive engine, unchanged in its assessment behaviour, but now **gated to only assess patterns that have been learned** and **no longer does any teaching itself**.

This makes the app's implicit blended model into an explicit **teach ‚Üí assess ‚Üí re-teach** loop.

### 19.2 The two modes (decided)
| | **Learn** (new) | **Test** (existing engine, trimmed) |
|---|---|---|
| Purpose | Explicit teaching | Adaptive assessment |
| Order | **Linear** ‚Äî one pattern at a time in fixed scope order | Adaptive rotation over *learned* patterns |
| Pressure | None ‚Äî no scoring gate, no timing, no difficulty ramp | Full engine (difficulty 1‚Äì3, mastery, SRS, readiness) |
| Unit | **One whole pattern, read + spell together** | Individual items |
| Teaching | Owns ALL teaching (intro lesson + guided practice + re-teach) | **None** ‚Äî teaching removed |
| "Done" signal | **Participation** ‚Äî completing the unit's steps marks the pattern **learned** | Mastery (existing dual-gate + retention) |
| Gating | A pattern can only be learned once its prereq pattern is learned | Only *learned* patterns are eligible |

### 19.3 Pattern status model + the linkage loop (decided)
A **pattern** = a phonics **decode** skill + its **encode** pair (`encodePairId`); identify a pattern by its **decode skill id** (e.g. `PH-cvc-short-vowels`). Threaded skills (`PH-letter-sounds`, `HF-words`) are NOT patterns ‚Äî leave them threaded/always-available as today.

Per (child, pattern) status, in order:
1. **not-started** ‚Äî never taught.
2. **learning** ‚Äî Learn unit in progress (transient).
3. **learned** ‚Äî Learn unit completed (or placement-credited). **Unlocks the pattern in Test.**
4. **mastered** ‚Äî Test confirmed mastery (existing dual-gate + retention ‚Üí certificate). Shown green in the Learn map.
5. **needs-review** ‚Äî Test detected struggle on a learned pattern ‚Üí flagged so **Learn resurfaces it** for re-teaching. Cleared when the child re-completes its Learn unit; Test then re-confirms.

**The loop:** Learn *learned* ‚Üí eligible in Test ¬∑ Test *mastery* ‚Üí mastered badge in Learn ¬∑ Test *struggle* ‚Üí needs-review ‚Üí Learn re-teaches ‚Üí learned again ‚Üí Test re-confirms.

### 19.4 Data model (DB v6)
Add ONE store (additive, oldVersion-guarded migration; bump `SCHEMA_VERSION` 5‚Üí6; add to `ALL_STORES` for export/import; wipe in `clearChildData`):
```
learn:{childId}  ‚Üí  rows keyed "childId::patternId"
  { patternId:string; learned:boolean; needsReview:boolean; learnedAt?:number; flaggedAt?:number }
```
- `mastered` is NOT stored here ‚Äî derive it from the existing `progress` store (`status:'mastered'`) / `certificates`, so there is one source of truth for mastery.
- Store helpers in `store.ts`: `getLearn(childId)`, `putLearn(childId, row)`, `setLearned(childId, patternId)`, `flagReview(childId, patternId)`, `clearReview(childId, patternId)`.

### 19.5 Engine / gating changes (`engine.ts`, `placement.ts`)
- **Gate Test by learned:** `eligibleSkills(attempts, pre, learnedPatterns)` ‚Äî a decode/encode skill is eligible only if its pattern (`patternDecodeSkill(s).id`) ‚àà `learnedPatterns` (threaded skills stay exempt). This is the single most important change: **Test never serves an unlearned pattern.**
- **Learn frontier:** add `nextToLearn(learnedPatterns)` ‚Üí the first pattern in scope order whose prereq pattern is learned (or has no prereq) and that is not itself learned. Drives Learn's linear order. Reuse `decodeLadder` (placement.ts) + the scope prereqs.
- **Placement marks learned:** `placement.priorSkillIds` already credits sub-frontier patterns as `mastered`; also mark those patterns **learned** (they're known). The held-back top-2 **encode** rungs (¬ß7 #3) ‚Üí their patterns are **decode-known but spelling-untaught**, so treat them as **not learned** ‚Üí they become the first real Learn units (Learn teaches the spelling; the read half can be a quick review).

### 19.6 Learn mode UX ‚Äî `LearnRunner` (new `src/features/LearnRunner.tsx`)
Linear walk. On entry, pick the target pattern = the first **needs-review** pattern (re-teach takes priority), else `nextToLearn`. One **unit** = one pattern, taught read+spell together:
1. **Intro / rule** ‚Äî the pattern's decode `getLesson(decodeSkillId)` (rule + worked examples; `LessonView`, audio-first). *I-do.*
2. **Read practice** ‚Äî a few `decode_choice` items at difficulty 1 (reuse `McqItem` + the ¬ß8 immediate error-correction). **No score kept, no pass bar** ‚Äî purely practice; the child just does them. *we-do ‚Üí you-do.*
3. **Spell rule + practice** ‚Äî the encode `getLesson(encodeSkillId)` then a few `build_word` items d1 (reuse `TileItem` + correction). *we-do ‚Üí you-do.*
4. **Mark learned** ‚Äî `setLearned(child, patternId)` + `clearReview` (both decode & encode now unlock in Test). Celebrate ("You learned **sh, ch, th, ck**! Try it in Test.").
5. **Continue** ‚Äî offer "Learn the next one" (‚Üí next `nextToLearn`) or "Done".

Rules: participation-based (finishing the steps = learned; no accuracy gate ‚Äî the per-item error-correction guarantees correct final production). No difficulty ramp, no timing, no SRS. Item counts per step are small/fixed (e.g. 3‚Äì4). Reuse existing pools via `pickItem(skillId, 1, seen)`.

### 19.7 Test mode changes (`Session.tsx`)
- **Remove teaching:** delete the first-exposure intro-lesson branch (the `introRef` block, ¬ß3 explicit-first ‚Äî that teaching now lives in Learn) and the struggle‚Üí`LessonView` branch. Keep the post-lesson **guided block** mechanism only insofar as it's reused by the down-shift; otherwise the lesson phase is gone from Test.
- **Struggle ‚Üí flag, don't teach:** when `struggling(...)` on a learned pattern, call `flagReview(child, patternId)` (sets needs-review) and keep going; optionally end the session early with a gentle "Let's practise **X** in Learn." **Keep the per-item immediate error-correction** (reveal + reproduce correct ‚Äî that's answer integrity, not a lesson) and the ¬ß7 #5 down-shift.
- **Gate eligibility:** load the child's `learned` set on mount; pass it to `eligibleSkills`. If nothing is learned yet, Test shows "Learn a pattern first" and routes to Learn.
- Everything else (difficulty, mastery dual-gate + retention, SRS, interleave, massed-first, fluency, readiness, aggregates/daily) is **unchanged**.

### 19.8 Placement interaction (`Placement.tsx`, `App.tsx`)
- Placement still runs once for a new child (warm-up staircase, ¬ß7). On finish it marks sub-frontier patterns **learned** + **mastered** (as today) and holds back the top-2 encode rungs (not learned). The child then lands on the **child picker**; from there they choose **Learn** (starts at their frontier) or **Test** (only their placement-learned patterns are available until they Learn more).
